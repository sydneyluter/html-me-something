A Journey in Food

The Beginning

Growing up, my family rarely ate at restaurants. Both of my parents introduced me to the joy of food and cooking from a very young age. My father was breakfast and grill-master (as many fathers are). My mother cooked most other meals from scratch, rotating through her recipe box which held my family's most beloved recipes.

International Flavor

With family and friends from all over the world, I was exposed to the rich spices and flavors of Asia and the Middle East early on. After the first taste of curry, I developed a love for the way texture, temperature, consistency, and flavor all combine to create a complex piece of art. I started watching every food show I could, from America's Test Kitchen, to The Barefoot Contessa, to Anthony Bourdain: Parts Unknown. I gleaned from those shows what technique I could and idolized the chefs and foodies they portrayed. My friends teased me for my obsession with Ina Garten (The Barefoot Contessa) and even bought me a signed cookbook of hers.

Sous Chef

My mother always included me in her process. When I was too young to handle a knife, she'd yell out the ingredient she needed and I'd rush to the pantry to find it. Then, as I grew older, I played a larger part in meal preparation, and eventually sometimes cooking for my family on my own. Once I grew up and moved out, I enjoyed exploring new flavors and cuisines I never had before and tried more and more complex cooking techniques. Though I've had no formal training, cooking is a passion of mine. There is no greater feeling than creating a dish that fills the bellies of the people you love.
